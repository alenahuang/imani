Definitions

Abstinence

To refrain from, to not engage in; restraint from indulging a desire for something, e.g. alcohol or sexual intercourse

Abuse

Using something wrongly; misuse; overdo.

Alcohol and Drugs

Impairs the thought process and decision making skills; suppresses the immune system; increases the risk of HIV infection.

Anal Intercourse

Inserting the penis into the anus of the sexual partner.

Denial

A refusal to believe or accept what has been proven to be true; not admitting to having participated in risky behaviors.

Genitals

The reproductive organs, especially the external sex organs; the testicles and penis of a male or the labia, clitoris, and vagina of a female.

Intercourse

Social interaction or verbal communications between individuals; sexual penetration between individuals that involves the genitalia of at least one person.

Masturbation

Self stimulation of one's own genitals; a natural response to the human sex drive.

Monogamous

Long term (years) sexual relationship in which both partners are faithful and committed the their health and well being.

Multiple Sex Partners

More than one sexual partner in a lifetime.

Oral Sex

Contact of the month or tongue with a partner's penis, vagina or anus.

Risk Behavior

An act or action that can harm a person's health or cause an HIV infection.

STD(Sexually Transmitted Disease)

Any disease that is transferred from one person to another during sexual activity. HIV, Syphilis, Gonorrhea, Chlamydia and Herpes are all examples. Although there is treatment and cures for Syphilis, Gonorrhea and Chlamydia, HIV and Herpes are not curable.

Semen

Clear or whitish sexual fluid seeped or ejaculated from the penis that contains sperm, white blood cells and fluid, contains HIV in an infected male.

Vaginal Fluid

That provide internal cleanliness, protective lubrication for sexual intercourse and during orgasm; contains HIV in an infected female.

Virginity

Having never participated in oral, anal or vaginal sex.
